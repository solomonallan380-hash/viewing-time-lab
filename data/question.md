1. Context Setting

To better understand the current situation and avoid duplicating prior work, I would ask:

- What information, such as viewing time, engagement, and completion rates, has previously been gathered from earlier workshops?
- Have any comparable tests or redesigned slides been conducted previously?
- Were there any prior conclusions or insights regarding the reasons behind participants' disengagement throughout the workshop?
- How were the control and treatment groups formed?
- Are there any known outside variables that can affect viewing time, such as participant background or the time of day?
- What instruments or techniques were employed to monitor engagement and viewing time?


2. Goal Alignment

To ensure the analysis is aligned with the stakeholder’s goals, I would ask:

- So, what’s the real goal behind reworking this workshop? Are we trying to get people more involved, or is it all about helping them actually learn better? 
- When we talk about “success,” what does that look like—are we chasing higher viewing time, or do things like retention and quiz scores matter more? 
- If people watch longer, does that automatically mean it's working, or are there other signs we need to pay attention to? And honestly, how much improvement counts as meaningful? Is a small bump enough, or are we expecting big leaps? 
- Also, it's worth asking: are there any trade-offs to consider, like maybe folks spend less time watching but understand the material better? When all'
said and done, how will we use what we learn from this to make decisions going forward?
